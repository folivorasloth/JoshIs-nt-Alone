# Josh 🌿
### A shapeless wanderer looking for his form.

> *"He doesn't have a formed shape, no body — he is shapeless.  
> A mixture of geometric forms, colors, silhouettes and sounds.  
> Josh is confused about his life, therefore his shape is confused  
> on how to be, how to be seen, how to exist."*

---

## What is Josh?

Josh is a 2D philosophical adventure game being built in Unity — 
where the choices you make don't punish or reward you.
They just reveal who you are.

You play as Josh, a shapeless wanderer drifting through a quiet world,
gathering food, meeting strangers, and slowly finding form
through music, connection, and the small decisions no one is watching.

There is no morality system. No score. No judgment.
Just you, Josh, and the world passing by.

---

## Core Philosophy

Most games tell you what a good choice looks like.

Josh doesn't.

When you decide whether to climb a tree and disturb the birds living there —
or walk away hungry to preserve their peace —
the game says nothing.

That silence is intentional.

**The player's choices reveal the player. Not the character.**

## Aesthetic

Josh lives somewhere between **Sassy the Sasquatch**, **Sludge Life**,  
and a field recording made at golden hour with birds disappearing into the wind.

Uncanny. Quiet. Honest.

Not too realistic. Not too cartoonish.  
Just strange enough to feel real.

---

## The Story (Scratch)

Josh is wandering through the woods, thinking about hunger.

Maybe he can climb a tree — but that would disturb the birds living there,  
and Josh doesn't want that.  
Maybe he can pick fruit from the ground — but what if it's poisonous?  

Josh just wants to end his hunger.

Along the way he finds a well with warm water, an old house with an old family  
and their old dog, and an old guitar leaning against a wall.

*"Yeah, definitely everything here is old,"* Josh thinks.

He picks up the guitar — after asking — and plays.  
The family cries.  
And for the first time, Josh starts to take shape.
